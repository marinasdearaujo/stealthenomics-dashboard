Detailed Strategy

1. Fix the Webinar Funnel

Last month: 1,372 clicks → 8 registrations (5 qualified). Sub-1% conversion rate. We've moved from RingCentral to Zoom with our own domain. Now we need full tracking:

  • Meta pixel on confirmation page — enables switching from Traffic to Conversions objective
  • UTM parameters on every link — pre-built per channel in a shared spreadsheet (Facebook organic, Facebook ad, Chris LinkedIn, outbound DM, email — each gets a unique tagged URL)
  • Form data → HubSpot — one place to track registration to attendance to sale

Webinar Scaling Plan

April20-50Pixel, UTMs, Conversions campaign, organic + outbound push

June80-100Full engine — paid + organic + community all contributing

2. Improve SMB Landing Page (Week 1 priority)

Every dollar we spend on Google Ads, webinar retargeting, and organic content is only as good as the page it lands on. The SMB landing page is the conversion point for the entire bottom of the funnel — it needs to work before we scale traffic to it.

Optimization priorities:

  • Clear value prop above the fold — What is the Launch Box, who is it for, and why should they care? Answer in 1-2 sentences.
  • Social proof — Chris's credibility (Forbes featured, COO story), client testimonials, specific results.
  • Stronger CTA — Clear next step (book a call, register for webinar, or apply). One primary action, not multiple competing options.
  • Qualifying form — Keep the qualifying questions (company size, timeline) but reduce friction on non-essential fields.
  • Mobile optimization — Most Meta/Google ad traffic arrives on mobile. The page must load fast and convert on small screens.
  • Pixel + tracking — Ensure Meta pixel and Google tag fire on form submission for conversion optimization.

Timeline: LP improvements should be completed in Week 1, before Google Ads launch. Sending paid traffic to an unoptimized page wastes budget.

3. Qualified Leads — Meta to Google

We tested two approaches on Meta for direct lead generation:

Instant forms → fake phone numbers, bot entries

Landing page conversion → low conversion rate

Conclusion: Meta's audience isn't in buying mode for direct lead gen. They're browsing, not searching. Move direct lead gen to Google Search where intent is real.

Google Ads Setup

  • Search campaigns: High-intent keywords — technology acquisition, managed IT, business launch consulting. 10-15 keywords, not broad match.
  • Lead form: Manual phone entry + qualifying questions (company size, timeline, challenge).
  • Display + Video: Retargeting only. No cold prospecting.
  • After 30 days: Compare contact rate and qualification rate vs Meta. Data-driven decision on permanent allocation.

Also: Connect CRM with Conversions API — Meta's #1 recommendation (score: 90/100). Estimated 19% lower cost per quality lead. Free to implement.

4. Organic: Focus on What's Working

✓ What works

  • LinkedIn company page: 14%+ ER
  • Facebook video: 14.3% ER
  • Relatable/pain point content: 20.3% ER on FB
  • Interactive posts: 17-37% ER
  • Chris's personal stories: #1 across all platforms
  • YouTube 16-60s videos: avg 1,434 views

✗ What doesn't work

  • Instagram images: 5-8 reach per post
  • Friday Facts: consistently 0 reactions
  • Cross-posting identical copy
  • YouTube long-form (60s+): avg 5 views
  • Company page only: 1.6% follower reach

Biggest untapped opportunity: Chris's personal LinkedIn. We've never posted there. Personal profiles get 561% more reach than company pages. Starting at 3x/week as a test — if it performs as expected, we scale up.

Free Diagnostic — Funnel Entry Point

The Free Business Diagnostic is a lower-commitment entry point than the webinar — no date/time required, available anytime. It captures a lead, reveals gaps in their business infrastructure, and creates a natural next step:

"Your score is 78%. Here are the 3 areas holding your business back. Want the full framework to fix them? Join our next live webinar — or join the community for ongoing support."

The diagnostic should be promoted as an alternative CTA on posts where the webinar isn't the right fit — educational content, value-first posts, and trend/reach content.

YouTube Strategy — Daily via Opus Clip + Showcases

  • Daily webinar cuts — Beverly uses Opus Clip to extract short clips (16-60s sweet spot) from webinar recordings and posts daily. Share Opus Clip login with Beverly.
  • Product showcases — BPC demos, Launch Box walkthroughs, client success stories. 1-2 per month as original content.
  • This feeds the YouTube algorithm with consistency (daily) without requiring new production. Our data shows 16-60s videos avg 1,434 views vs 5 for long-form.

Account Warming — Beverly

Beverly engages daily with relevant pages, posts, and profiles on LinkedIn and Facebook to warm up the StealthEnomics accounts. This signals the algorithm that the accounts are active and increases visibility of our own content. Commenting, sharing, and reacting to industry content — not automated, genuine engagement.

Boost the Winner Strategy

Post organically → after 24-48h, boost the best performer ($15/day, 3-5 days) → viewers enter retarget pool → retarget with webinar CTA ($15/day). Cost: $0.01-0.10/view on boost vs $5-8/registration cold.

6. Outbound: LinkedIn Personal Invitations

For a high-ticket offer like the Launch Box, personal outreach from Chris can be more effective than any ad:

  • Target list: Founders, CEOs, owners of 1-50 employee companies. LinkedIn search filters by industry, size, geography.
  • Sequence: Personalized connection note (not a pitch) → share value/content → personal webinar invitation after engagement.
  • Volume: 10-20 connections/week. At 10% conversion = 4-8 registrations/month — hand-picked and free.
  • Compounding: Every connection stays in Chris's network for future webinars. Every post Chris makes reaches this growing audience.

A Note on Focus

This plan is designed for a 2-person team (~12-16 hours/week recurring + 12-16 hours setup in weeks 1-2).

  • Chris's LinkedIn 3x/week (test) — LinkedIn-native formats
  • Company LinkedIn 5x/week (Beverly)
  • YouTube daily — Opus Clip webinar cuts + product showcases (Beverly)
  • Facebook + Instagram 3x/week each

What we're NOT doing

  • Instagram native content (repurpose from FB/YT)
  • Friday Facts or low-performing formats
  • Cross-posting identical copy across platforms
  • Meta direct lead gen (tested, didn't work)
  • Google Display cold prospecting
  • Video on LinkedIn (underperforms — save for FB/YT)

The biggest risk isn't the strategy — it's scope creep. Every platform or request added mid-month takes time directly from this funnel. If scope expands, we should revisit the targets together.
